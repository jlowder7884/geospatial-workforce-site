The Geospatial Workforce Institute helps schools introduce students to land surveying through a structured program designed to build career awareness, practical knowledge, and CST Level 1 readiness.

  • ✔ Career-connected learning
  • ✔ CST Level 1 aligned instruction

Why This Matters for Schools

Schools are being asked to prepare students for meaningful careers, but many high-value professions remain invisible to the students who could thrive in them. Land surveying is one of them.

Expands Career Awareness

Students are introduced to a profession connected to construction, infrastructure, land development, and geospatial technology.

Strengthens CTE Pathways

The program gives schools a practical way to expand workforce-connected learning without having to build a new pathway from scratch.

Connects Learning to Opportunity

Students gain a clearer understanding of how classroom learning can lead directly to real careers and future advancement.

The Need Is Real

The surveying profession faces a growing workforce shortage as experienced professionals retire faster than new talent enters the pipeline. Schools have an opportunity to help close that gap.

The challenge

Students often graduate without ever being exposed to surveying or understanding that it is a viable, technology-connected career with real long-term value.

At the same time, industry continues to need people with foundational awareness, practical understanding, and a clear entry point into the profession.

The opportunity for schools

By introducing land surveying as part of a broader workforce strategy, schools can help students discover overlooked careers while strengthening the relevance of CTE instruction.

This is not just a content opportunity. It is a pipeline opportunity.
