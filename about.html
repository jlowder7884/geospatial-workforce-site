About the Geospatial Workforce Institute

The Geospatial Workforce Institute exists to help students, schools, and communities connect with the future of surveying and geospatial technology through practical, workforce-focused education.

Our Purpose

We are building a modern pathway into a profession that is essential to construction, infrastructure, land development, mapping, and public works, yet too often overlooked by students and underserved by traditional education pipelines.

Our Mission

To prepare the next generation of land surveying and geospatial professionals by creating accessible, relevant, and workforce-aligned learning experiences.

We aim to make this field visible, understandable, and achievable for students who are ready for meaningful careers connected to technology, problem-solving, and the built environment.

Our Vision

We envision a future where schools, students, and industry partners work together to rebuild the talent pipeline for surveying and geospatial careers.

The Geospatial Workforce Institute is designed to become a bridge between classroom learning and real-world opportunity.

Why This Matters

Surveying and geospatial technology support the work that shapes our communities. Roads, utilities, construction sites, land boundaries, mapping systems, and infrastructure projects all depend on skilled professionals who understand how to measure, interpret, and manage the physical world.

Industry Demand

The profession is facing a workforce shortage as experienced surveyors retire faster than new professionals are entering the field.

Student Opportunity

Students deserve access to high-value career pathways that lead to real jobs, real skills, and long-term professional growth.

School Impact

Schools and CTE programs need scalable ways to introduce career-connected learning that aligns with both workforce needs and student success.

What We Believe

Strong workforce education should be practical, modern, and connected to the real world.

Students learn best when education is relevant

  • Hands-on learning creates stronger understanding.
  • Career context increases motivation and engagement.
  • Clear pathways help students see a future for themselves.

Schools need flexible, high-quality solutions

  • Programs should be practical to implement.
  • Instruction should reflect real industry expectations.
  • Technology should support teachers, not replace them.

Built for the Future

The Geospatial Workforce Institute is being developed with a long-term view: scalable online learning, workforce alignment, modern instructional design, and AI-supported student learning tools that can help make education more effective and accessible.

Career Exploration

Helping students discover surveying and geospatial careers they may never have known existed.

Workforce Readiness

Building foundational knowledge and habits that connect directly to industry needs.

Scalable Delivery

Creating a model that can grow from a single classroom to multiple schools, programs, and partnerships.

Founder’s Perspective

This institute is being built by an educator who sees both the classroom challenge and the workforce opportunity.

Education with purpose

The Geospatial Workforce Institute was created from the belief that students need more than information. They need direction, opportunity, and a clear connection between what they learn today and what they can become tomorrow.

By combining classroom experience, industry awareness, and a practical vision for workforce education, this institute is designed to help students step into careers that matter and help schools build programs with lasting value.

Building the Next Generation of Geospatial Talent

The Geospatial Workforce Institute is more than a course platform. It is a workforce pathway, a school partnership model, and a long-term investment in the future of surveying and geospatial education.
